I am Zi Huang, a PhD student at the Department of Translation and Language Sciences at Universitat Pompeu Fabra. I am a member of GLiF, or Grup de Lingüística Formal ("Formal Linguistics Research Group" in Catalan). I'm currently writing my PhD thesis under the supervision of Louise McNally. I work in formal semantics and natural language ontology, and my thesis focuses on the interpretation of two verbal gerund nominalizations in English (Zi's writing her thesis vs Zi writing her thesis). Here is my CV.

My first name can be a bit tricky to pronounce, but a dzzzz noise will do the job. One of my biggest passions is decoding messages, and this leads me into constantly learning new languages. I am a native speaker of Mandarin and Wu Chinese, work in English, Spanish and Catalan, and read some German, French, Italian and Esperanto.
